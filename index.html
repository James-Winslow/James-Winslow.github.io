About Me

Hi, I'm Jimmy and I currently live in the Seattle area. My journey in the field of data science and statistics has been a rich and fulfilling adventure, marked by a passion for uncovering truths through data.

I began my academic career at Gonzaga University, where I pursued a dual degree in Mathematics (BS) and Biology (BA). The rigorous curriculum and diverse subjects ignited my curiosity and equipped me with a solid foundation in quantitative analysis and scientific research. After completing my undergraduate studies, I dedicated a year to volunteer service at the Tennessee Justice Center. Here, I worked as a Community Outreach Coordinator, where I educated underinsured and uninsured clients about insurance eligibility. This experience honed my skills in communication and advocacy, and deepened my understanding of the social impacts of healthcare policies.

Driven by a desire to specialize further, I enrolled in the University of Colorado Denver's graduate program, where I earned my Master of Public Health (MPH) in Biostatistics. This program refined my ability to analyze complex data sets and reinforced my commitment to public health and biostatistics.

My professional journey began at the VA Eastern Colorado Health Care System as a Statistician. I conducted analyses of longitudinal and survival data from clinical studies, which expanded the number of active studies for our biostatistics team by 20%. I developed analytic plans and reports for physicians in various specialties, and I became adept at using SQL, SAS, and R to manage and analyze data from diverse sources. I then transitioned to the University of Colorado School of Medicine, where I worked as a Data Scientist. I designed and deployed an R Shiny application to evaluate surgical risk, which significantly increased its user base among surgical partners. Through collaboration and feedback integration, I improved the surgical risk prediction model, reducing errors by 15%.

Most recently, I served as a Data Scientist at Arisant, where I designed and implemented an analysis pathway for the Bureau of Safety and Environmental Enforcement, reducing errors by 15%. I managed diverse databases, built custom data pipelines, and created machine learning models to predict customer churn, enhancing client success and retention.

As I continue to develop my data science and statistics skills, my goal is to make a meaningful contribution to my field, particularly in the realm of AI and large language models (LLMs). I am passionate about discovering the truth and combating misinformation through rigorous data analysis. I hope to leverage my expertise to help both the public and private sectors approach data with integrity and precision. I am eager to stay at the forefront of the rapidly evolving data landscape and to use my skills to make a positive impact. Whether through advancing public health initiatives or driving innovation in technology, my commitment to uncovering truth and promoting data-driven decision-making remains steadfast.
