About

The longer version.

I'm a data scientist with a background in biostatistics, public health, and mathematics — which is a roundabout way of saying I've spent a lot of time turning messy, high-stakes data into decisions that actually matter. I've built predictive models for healthcare systems, maintained clinical research pipelines for the VA, and helped government partners make sense of data they didn't know they had.

What makes me useful isn't just the technical range (Python, R, SQL, Bayesian modeling, causal inference, NLP) — it's that I care about communication as much as computation. A model no one understands is just expensive decoration. I write clearly, think in systems, and try to make every deliverable — whether it's a dashboard or a slide deck — as clear as the analysis behind it.

Outside of work, I'm somewhere on a Colorado 14er, deep in a philosophy book, or building side projects that probably cross too many genres. I believe strongly in finishing things, asking hard questions, and designing systems that actually hold up — whether that's a statistical model or a personal knowledge system.
